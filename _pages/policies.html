Policies

Participation in the micro-purchase experiment allows individuals and vendors of all types to bid on and complete open source software work for the federal government. If you’re interested in participating, here is some helpful information to get you ready!

Eligibility

  • Have a GitHub account.
  • All potential bidders must have a GitHub account in order to register for the micro-purchase platform. Users will log into the platform by authenticating with their GitHub account.
  • Register in SAM.gov.

SAM.gov is the federal System for Award Management (SAM). At the moment, we are only permitting companies registered in SAM.gov to actually deliver work on a micro-purchase task. In order to register with SAM, you first must have an active Data Universal Numbering System (DUNS) number and a Taxpayer Identification Number (TIN). If you do not have an active DUNS number, you may obtain one online at the Dun & Bradstreet site. This process takes one-to-two business days. If you do not have a TIN, you may complete an online application on the Tax ID site.

You can learn more about SAM in their User Guide or call their help desk at 1-866-606-8220.

Source-selection method

We’re using a reverse auction that will take place online on our Micro-purchase Platform. The person or company with the lowest bid at the end of the auction will be the winner.

Bid acknowledgement and communication

18F will contact the winning bidder within 24 hours of the end of the auction. We require a timely response to acknowledge and confirm the bid amount. The code requested in the auction will be due as explained in the requirements of each specific auction.

The requirements must be delivered within the time alloted for each auction. The countdown to the deadline begins as soon as the auction ends.
